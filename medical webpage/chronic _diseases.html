Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) Information

Symptoms of COPD

  • Shortness of breath, especially during physical activity
  • Chronic cough with or without mucus production
  • Wheezing or whistling sound when breathing
  • Tightness in the chest
  • Frequent respiratory infections
  • Lack of energy and fatigue
  • Unintended weight loss
  • Swelling in the ankles, feet, or legs

Precautions for COPD Management

  • Avoid exposure to tobacco smoke and quit smoking if you are a smoker
  • Avoid exposure to indoor and outdoor air pollutants
  • Follow prescribed treatment plans, including medication and therapy
  • Practice breathing exercises and pulmonary rehabilitation
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle with regular exercise and a balanced diet
  • Get vaccinated against respiratory infections like influenza and pneumonia
  • Avoid respiratory irritants and triggers, such as strong chemicals or allergens
  • Monitor and manage symptoms, and seek medical attention for any exacerbations
  • Create a safe and supportive environment at home, reducing risks of falls and accidents
  • Stay informed and work closely with healthcare professionals for ongoing care and management
