Respiratory Disease Information

Common Symptoms of Respiratory Diseases

  • Coughing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest tightness or pain
  • Wheezing or noisy breathing
  • Sputum production (phlegm or mucus)
  • Fever or chills
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Headache

Precautions for Respiratory Disease Prevention

  • Practice good hand hygiene by washing hands frequently with soap and water or using hand sanitizer
  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick or showing symptoms of respiratory illnesses
  • Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when coughing or sneezing
  • Wear a mask in crowded or high-risk settings, especially during disease outbreaks
  • Avoid touching your face, especially your eyes, nose, and mouth
  • Keep a distance from others, especially if they are coughing, sneezing, or not wearing a mask
  • Maintain a clean and well-ventilated environment at home and in public spaces
  • Follow vaccination guidelines and get immunized against common respiratory infections
  • Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Adopt a healthy lifestyle with regular exercise, balanced diet, and sufficient sleep
