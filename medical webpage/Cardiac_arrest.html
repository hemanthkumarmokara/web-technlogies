Cardiac Arrest Information

Symptoms of Cardiac Arrest

  • Sudden loss of responsiveness
  • No normal breathing or gasping
  • No pulse or heartbeat
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Chest pain or discomfort (may occur before cardiac arrest)

Precautions for Cardiac Arrest

  • Call emergency services immediately for help
  • Start performing CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation) if trained
  • Use an automated external defibrillator (AED) if available and follow instructions
  • Do not delay in initiating CPR or using an AED
  • If a trained person is available, continue performing CPR until medical professionals arrive
  • Encourage bystanders to locate and bring an AED if one is nearby
  • Avoid excessive movement of the person experiencing cardiac arrest
  • Follow the guidance of medical professionals and emergency responders
  • Learn CPR and basic life support techniques
  • Stay informed about cardiac health and risk factors
