Electrical Injuries Information

Symptoms of Electrical Injuries

  • Burns or charred skin
  • Pain at the site of the shock
  • Muscle contractions or spasms
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Irregular heartbeat or chest pain
  • Seizures or loss of consciousness

Precautions to Prevent Electrical Injuries

  • Always turn off the power before working with electrical appliances or wiring
  • Use insulated tools and equipment
  • Wear personal protective equipment (such as gloves and safety goggles) when working with electricity
  • Avoid using electrical appliances near water
  • Keep electrical cords in good condition and avoid overloading outlets
  • Ensure proper grounding of electrical systems
  • Use childproof outlet covers to prevent accidental shocks
  • Regularly inspect electrical systems and consult professionals for repairs or maintenance
